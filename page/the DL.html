和平號

  這艘船是荷蘭拓展東印度群島船隊的一艘，1600年6月天氣惡劣，船隻四散，和平號(DeLiefde)因而漂流到日本豐後水道，當時航海長—英人威廉亞當斯在船上。

  亞當斯不久後拜見德川家康，稟告德川家康將軍其航海經驗，使德川家康德以拓展世界知識並因此聘他為外交事務顧問，為其取日本名「三浦按針」，「按針」即「航海家」之意。 亞當斯並且教德川家康數學和幾何學，亦曾在伊東地方建造80噸級的西洋帆船，對日本與外國接觸有重大貢獻。




The "De Liefde"

  As one vessel of a Dutch fleet exploring a route to the East India, "De Liefde" drifted to Wengo Suido in Japan in June 1600, as a result of inclement weather which dispersed the fleet. The chief of navigation, William Adams who was English, was on board.

  Adams soon had an audience with Shogun Tokugawa.  He informed the general of his experience in navigation which broadened the Shogun's knowledge of the world.  Tokugawa Ieyasu, therefore, employed him as an adviser in foreign affairs and gave him a Japanese name, "Miura Anjin". "Anjin" means "pilot".

  William Adams also taught him mathematics, and geometry.  In return, the general invited him to build a Western sailboat of 80 tons in Idon.  Historically speaking, "De Liefde", therefore, can be regarded as a ship which contributed much to the development of Japan.
