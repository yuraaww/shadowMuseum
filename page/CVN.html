尼米茲核子動力航空母鑑

  美國尼米茲核子動力航空母鑑是歷史上最大的軍艦，也是最昂貴的軍艦，建造這樣的巨無霸是為了要充分運用F-14熊貓式等90架高性能戰鬥機。

該艦載機數量一覽：
F-14熊貓式戰機2個中隊--20架
F-18 A 大黃蜂式攻擊機2個中隊--20架
A-6 入侵者式攻擊機2個中隊--20架
S-3 A 維京人式反潛機1個中隊--10架
SH-3 H 海王式反潛直昇機1個中隊--6架
EA-6 B 徘徊者電子反制機1個分遣隊--6架
E-2 C 鷹眼空中雷達預警機1個分遣隊--5架
C-2 A 灰狗式艦載運輸機--3架

  尼米茲航空母艦全長332.9公尺，寬48公尺，吃水11.3公尺，滿載排水量91,487噸，輕負荷排水量72,798噸，標準排水量81,600噸，戰鬥機排水量93,400噸。 艦上有四座蒸氣渦輪驅動的螺旋槳四支，使這龐然大物以超過30浬以上的速度移動。

  飛行甲板離吃水線約14公尺，從艦底到飛行甲板是一體構成，艦載機機棚密閉在船體內，僅在兩舷側有開口四處（右舷側三處，左舷側一處），供飛機專用升降機使用，並可以滑動式門扇密閉。 尼米茲的機棚長209公尺，寬33公尺，高8.1公尺，相當於三層甲板的高度。 飛行甲板有16,200平方公尺，約5,000坪，以供90架高性能飛機起降及停機之用。 飛行甲板採用平面型，自1961年小鷹號CV-63完工以來，成為美國航空母艦的標準型式，艦橋前方有二座飛機用升降機，後方一座，左舷側後方一座，左前方到右後方的斜角飛行甲板是供飛機降落使用。 起飛位置在飛行甲板前半部和斜角飛行甲板前半部，各安置二座飛機彈射器。

  飛機從機棚到飛行甲板，須以專用升降機搬運。 尼米茲的升降機每座有374平方公尺，載重50噸，可同時載動A-6及A-7攻擊機。 來回機棚與飛行甲板間，需時一分鐘。

  艦機以升降機送到飛行甲板後，停在艦橋左側及前後的停機甲板，裝載武器，完成出擊準備，即利用飛行甲板前半部或斜角飛行甲板的彈射器彈射起飛。 裝載尼米茲的彈射器系C-13-1型，彈射力達970萬公尺/公斤，可將30噸重飛機在76.3公尺起飛距離內，由時速0加速到256公里，這樣的彈射器裝有4座。

  艦載機起飛時需要輔助設施，降落時亦然；幫助降落的裝置叫做降落制止裝置。 其方法是進入降落區即將降落的飛機方下捕捉鉤，鉤住阻攔索，拖著阻攔索而停止，阻攔索是利用油壓汽缸，一面伸長一面吸收衝力，其吸收力約有690萬公尺/公斤，可使30噸重的噴射機滑行100公尺後停止。 阻攔索在美國航空母艦上的標準是4條，安置在斜角甲板後部降落區，即飛行甲板後端55公尺處起，以12公尺的間隔橫列。

  1960年代末期，發展出利用雷達完全自動的降落引擎裝置，但艦載機駕駛員仍須有目視降落的技能，因此也須利用光學降落引導裝置。 掌握所有艦載機起降作業的是位在艦橋最上段的主飛航管制塔，此處對整個飛行甲板狀況一目了然，而且重要區域都以閉路電視監視。

  美國航空母艦有 E-2C早期預警機 4-5架，保持將近百分之百的出動率，並另裝置艦上早期預警雷達，目前以三度空間的 SPS-48及二度空間長程型 SPS-49 為主力，還裝置有水面搜索用的 SPS-10F，航行用的 LN-66，降落引導用的 SPN-41/42/44，低空警戒用的 SPS-65，電子戰用的SLQ-32等各式雷達和收訊用天線，這些裝備都配有在艦橋周圍。

  航空母艦不能單獨行動，必須由巡洋艦、驅逐艦展開防衛掩護網，加以護衛，但是萬一有突破掩護網衝進來的敵人，尤其反艦飛彈時，就必須有防禦武器來抵抗。 其特點為防備反艦飛彈而配置的單艦武裝有北約海麻雀短程對空飛彈8連裝發射架3座，及20公釐火神式方陣近距防禦機炮 (CIWS)3-4門，海麻雀飛彈是連動於 SPS-65 低空預警雷達，又 SLQ-32(V)3可以發出干擾電波，以擾亂反艦飛彈的自動雷達歸航導引裝置，對抗紅外線導引飛彈，可以由 MK36 超級 RBOC 金屬箔片火箭發射裝置發射紅外線引誘照明彈。

  此模型船比例為1：100，約4公尺長。




NIMITZ CLASS AIRCRAFT CARRIERS (CVN)

DISPLACEMENT : 91,487 tons full load (CVN-71, 96, 358).
LENGTH : 1,040 feet.
BEAM : 134 feet.
FLIGHT DECK WIDTH : 252 feet.
SPEED : 30-plus knots.
POWER PLANT : two nuclear reactors, four geared steam turbines, four shafts, 280,000 shaft horsepower.
AIRCRAFT : 90-plus.
ARMAMENT : Sea Sparrow missiles, Phalanx CIWSS-three on Nimitz and Eisenhower, four on Vinson, four to be installed on later ships of class.
COMPLEMENT : 3,022 ship's company; 2,279 in air wing.
BUILDER: Newport News Shipbuilding.

  US nuclear-powered aircraft carrier class.  As a result of the expense involved in building the world's first nuclear-powered aircraft carrier, USS Enterprise, launched in 1960, the US Navy reverted to oil-fired boilers for its next two carriers, America and John F Kennedy.

  In 1965 a design contract was awarded to Newport News, the only US shipyard capable of building such vessels, for the design of a new nuclear-propelled carrier, powered by only two Westinghouse A4W pressurized-water-cooled nuclear reactors.

  The new carrier, to be named Nimitz (CVN. 68) was ordered in Fiscal Year 1967 and laid down at Newport News on June 22, 1968.  Problems with the nuclear reactors delayed her completion by almost two years, and she was finally launched on May 13, 1972 and commissioned three years later on May 3, 1975.  A second ship, Dwight D Eisenhower, (CVN. 69), was authorized in FY-1970 and laid down on August 14, 1970.  Her completion was also delayed by reactor problems, and she was not launched until October 11, 1975, and was commissioned on October 10, 1977.  The third member of the class, Carl Vinson (CVN. 70), was laid down on October 11, 1975.  Originally due to be launched in March 1979 and commissioned in 1981, her completion has been delayed by contractual problems between the builders and the navy.

  The design of the Nimitz Class was closely based on that of the America, with three starboard deck-edge lifts, two forward and one aft of the superstructure, and another aft on the port side, though the absence of necessity for exhaust uptakes permits a smaller island.  Four steam catapults are provided for the two flight decks. No sonar is fitted, though they are equipped with the Naval Tactical Data System and SPS-10 surface search and SPS-43A two-dimensional and SPS-48 three-dimensional air search radars.  Normal aircraft complement of up to 100 includes two fighter squadrons of F-4 Phantoms or F-14 Tomcats, two attack squadrons of A-7 Corsairs and one medium attack squadron of A-16 Intruders plus S-3 Viking antisubmarine aircraft, SH-3 Sea King A/S helicopters (to be replaced by the new Lamps helicopter), and RA-5C Vigilante reconnaissance, EA-6B Prowler and E-2 Hawkeye AEW and KA-6 tanker aircraft.  Armament comprises three Basic Point Defence Missile System launchers with Sea Sparrow missiles.  With their nuclear cores estimated to need replacement only once every 13 years, these vessels have a theoretical maximum range of up to one million miles at full speed.

  Since entering service the Nimitz has been assigned to the US 6th Fleet in the Atlantic, her first deployment being in a naval exercise in July 1976.  Since then she has participated in a number of US Navy and NATO exercises.  Dwight D Eisenhower had not been assigned by the end of 1978.

  The Nimitz Class, the largest warships in the world, are intended to serve well into the twenty-first century, with a projected life of 45 years.  They were planned to be augmented by further members, with authorization planned for a fourth member in FY-1979 and a fifth two years later, in order to maintain a minimum force of 12 aircraft carriers (the navy had planned for 14 or 15), but the US government's desire to reduce defense expenditure has led to alternative designs being prepared for smaller carriers under the project title CVNX.  These would carry about two-thirds the number of aircraft maintained by the Nimitz, and would presumably carry the new McDonnell Douglas F-18 Hornet rather than the more powerful but prohibitively expensive F-14 Tomcat.  There would also be little room for antisubmarine helicopters or even fixed-wing A/S aircraft.  However, the work involved in preparing a new design would result in the cost of these smaller and less capable carriers being not much less than that of the Nimitz Class ships -approximately $2,000 million each.
