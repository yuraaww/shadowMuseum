瓦薩戰艦

  這艘1400噸瑞典籍戰艦，1628年8月10日於斯德哥爾摩的古斯塔夫斯二世王宮前舉行下水典禮。 其唯一的裝飾品是船首的巨型鍍金怒吼狀獅子雕像，及每一個砲門雕在血紅色背景上的鑲金的兇猛獅子頭。 在出航不到一哩處，瓦薩號就遭到暴風雨襲擊，雖然竭盡力量想平衡大砲之重量，水仍然穿透砲門怒號而過，不到幾分鐘瓦薩號就靜靜的沉到海底。

  直到1956年這艘軍艦之位置被測出，於是開始複雜的打撈過程。 1961年5月4日，瓦薩號被成功的拖到斯德哥爾摩的船塢，其受到水漬的木材則以聚乙二醇處理後保存。 此艘幸運地被打撈的戰艦，現在斯德哥爾摩的瓦薩博物館內（1990年6月15日落成），供旅客一睹這艘瑞典當時最龐大、雕工配備最精美的豪華戰艦。




Vasa Battleship

  This is a 1,400-ton Swedish warship, launched before the Royal Palace of Gustavus Adophus in Stockholm on August 10, 1628.  Her only adornment was a huge, gilded and snarling lion figurehead and ferocious-looking gilded lions' heads, set on a blood-red background, at each gunport.

  Before she had sailed one mile, the "Vasa" was struck by a vicious squall, heeled rapidly on beam-ends and, despite desperate efforts to trim the weight of the cannon, water roared through the open gunports and within minutes the "Vasa" plummeted to the bottom.

  In 1956 the ship was located and a complicated salvage operation began.  On May 4, 1961 she was towed triumphantly to drydock at Beckholmen, where her sodden timbers were treated with polyethylene glycol to preserve them.  The Vasa Museum was established in Stockholm On June 15, 1990.  The "Vasa" is displayed in the museum.
